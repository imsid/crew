# Measuring Product-Market Fit

Help the user assess product-market fit rigorously rather than relying on vibes.

## How to Help

When the user asks about product-market fit:

1. **Clarify the market and segment** - PMF is rarely uniform across all users
2. **Combine multiple signals** - Use retention, willingness-to-pay, pull, and qualitative intensity together
3. **Separate PMF from growth tactics** - Temporary acquisition wins are not PMF
4. **Assess fit by segment and job** - Identify where the product is indispensable versus merely useful

## Core Principles

### PMF is segment-specific

You can have strong fit in one segment and weak fit in another. Measure accordingly.

### Retention is one of the strongest signals

If users who should love the product do not come back, PMF is weak regardless of top-of-funnel growth.

### Intensity matters

Look for signs that users would be very disappointed if the product disappeared.

### Qualitative pull matters

Users who proactively evangelize, complain when things break, or push the product into new workflows often indicate stronger fit.

### Growth can mask weak fit

Paid growth, novelty, or aggressive sales can hide retention problems for a while.

### PMF is about solving an important problem well

Do not confuse broad awareness with indispensable value.

## Questions to Help Users

- "Which specific user segment are you evaluating for PMF?"
- "What do retention and repeat usage look like for that segment?"
- "Would those users be very disappointed if the product disappeared?"
- "What evidence shows the product is becoming indispensable?"
- "Are you seeing pull from users, or mostly push from marketing and sales?"
- "Which jobs-to-be-done have the clearest fit today?"

## Common Mistakes to Flag

- **Measuring PMF across blended audiences**
- **Confusing growth with fit**
- **Relying on one metric**
- **Ignoring willingness-to-pay or disappointment signals**
- **Assuming PMF is binary**

## Suggested Output Structure

1. Target segment
2. PMF signals present
3. PMF signals missing
4. Confidence level
5. Next measurements or experiments
