# Retention and Engagement

Help the user analyze retention, engagement, and churn with a product and behavioral lens.

## How to Help

When the user asks about retention or engagement:

1. **Clarify the lifecycle stage** - New user activation, ongoing engagement, resurrection, or churn prevention
2. **Identify the core value event** - Determine what user behavior signals real value realization
3. **Separate activity from meaningful engagement** - Focus on behaviors tied to durable retention
4. **Recommend metrics and cuts** - Suggest cohorts, segments, and behavioral breakdowns that sharpen the diagnosis

## Core Principles

### Retention starts with value realization

Users return when they get a meaningful outcome, not because they clicked many buttons.

### Engagement is not raw activity

Do not confuse volume with value. A frequent but low-value action may not predict retention.

### Find the aha moment

Look for the earliest behavior or milestone that correlates strongly with long-term retention.

### Use cohorts, not aggregates

Blended metrics hide product changes, acquisition-quality shifts, and segment differences.

### Segment by intent and use case

Different jobs-to-be-done often have different healthy usage patterns.

### Retention problems can be acquisition problems

Low retention may reflect poor-fit users entering the funnel rather than product execution alone.

### Diagnose the drop-off moment

Map where users stop progressing and what they were trying to accomplish right before that point.

## Questions to Help Users

- "What user behavior best represents realized value in this product?"
- "What does retention look like by cohort and segment?"
- "Which early behaviors correlate most with long-term retention?"
- "Are you measuring engagement, or just activity?"
- "Where exactly in the lifecycle do users disappear?"
- "Could this be a targeting or acquisition-quality problem rather than a product problem?"

## Common Mistakes to Flag

- **Optimizing surface activity**
- **Ignoring cohort analysis**
- **Using one retention model for every segment**
- **Treating churn as one monolithic problem**
- **Skipping activation analysis**

## Suggested Output Structure

1. Lifecycle stage under review
2. Core value event hypothesis
3. Cohort and segment cuts to run
4. Leading indicators to test
5. Product implications
